Research Interests

    Currently engaged in semiconductor device simulation and modeling:
  • Developing in-house quantum transport simulation programs and numerical solvers based on the k·p-NEGF framework. [more about k·p-NEGF Programs]
  • Utilizing advanced TCAD tools and atomistic simulation software (e.g., Sentaurus TCAD, QuantumATK) to investigate the quantum transport behavior and device physics of silicon-based nano-devices, including FinFETs and Gate-All-Around (GAA) architectures.
  • Investigating quantum transport mechanisms about spintronics devices, with a particular focus on conventional magnetic tunnel junctions (MTJs), antiferromagnetic tunnel junctions, and emerging altermagnetic tunnel junctions.
    • During my Master's degree in Nanjing University:
  • focused on computational condensed matter physics, utilizing computational methods (First-principles calculations + Tight-binding + 𝐤 ⋅𝐩 approximation) to investigate various material properties (topological materials), such as electrical and magnetic properties.
